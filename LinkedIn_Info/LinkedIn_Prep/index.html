    Overview

    This section of the site has been created as a shared section with students learning LinkedIn and Handshake for the first time.

    LinkedIn

    Before you start:

    • Purpose and Goals
    • Target Audience
    • Personal Brand w/ Images and Messaging
    • Management Routine
    • Searching and Networking Strategies

    Points

    Key sections in regards to the algorithm and this will help your profile come up higher in searches and therefore more likely to get a job.

    How to add points:

    • Connections of up to 500 (more doesn't impact points) will add some points
    • Posting activities - suggestion is 1x per week. (Reposts with 4+ original words count as a post)

    LinkedIn Core Elements

    Create a professional portrait, doesn't really need to be professional dress, from there have a background, add a headline, and write core sections: about, experience, education, and skills.

    • Headlines: put in current role, previous roles, different roles you're interested in, key skills (common in the job postings), or specialized skills - 240 Characters
      • The headline is an important section that should contain high search elements.
    • Experience - have your thoughts on the role
    • Recommendations - come from connections and look good to hiring teams. Ask: Professors, teammates, Bosses and even friends.
    • Projects - Add them direct and you can add them to your portfolio. Maybe portfolio's have a sub section which includes projects not on the front page.
    • About Section - 2600 characters Your story (why you entered the path), goals, skills focus, then close with an invitation to connect.
    • Add skills from across your experience and these skills can be soft skills.
    • Follow companies that interest you and you can search roles there that you'd like to connect with those individuals.

    These elements also transfer over to Handshake but are under slightly different names.

    Handshake

    Setup very similar to LinkedIn, really they are very similar, and since they are similar why would you have both? Well Handshake is more of a closed system that companies have to pay to be involved in, and then they have to pay more to in ASU's group. Therefore, most of the companies are really looking to make a return and will hire people which means in school this is likely your best bet to get a job while in school or fresh out.

    Do you have access to handshake? - Students at ASU do have access to handshake and should already have an account.

    Can I get someone to check over either profile or other job material? YES, ASU Career Services are available to both current and alumni students. They have appointments where you will be able to get specific information regarding your profile, resumes, or even practice interviews. Are they helpful? Well that fully depends on how you use them and how you seek help. For me, they offered knowledge about the algorithm and a few key points that I could improve my LinkedIn page.
